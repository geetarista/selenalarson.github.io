About

Selena is a tech reporter for ReadWrite in San Francisco.

Previously, she was a freelance journalist and multimedia producer working as a host for creativeLIVE, writing for publications including VentureBeat, AZ Tech Beat and SheKnows, and as an editorial professional helping small businesses tell their story online. Her work has appeared in Lifehacker, The Arizona Republic, Business Insider, and others.

She has extensive background in both print and digital media and has worked for the Downtown Chandler Community Partnership, FOX Sports Arizona, and Banner Health. She cofounded the magazine Gateway to the East Valley with her best friend and mentor Jennifer Lindley, and served as the assistant editor for over a year.

In her spare time, she is writing a book, a series on Medium, and occasionally updating her travel blog, Seeking Cities. Her award-winning multimedia pieces include Child Slaves of West Africa, Scars of War and Living the American Dream.

She was an anchor at Gangplank, a collaborative workspace in Chandler, Ariz. where she volunteered her time as a public relations consultant for startups and led their student STEM program, GangplankJr.

A recent transplant from Arizona, she is desperately in need of more jackets. She hates writing about herself in the third person.
